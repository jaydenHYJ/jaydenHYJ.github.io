I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Institute of Computational Mechanics X AI, Fudan University. My research focuses on computational mechanics, fatigue life prediction, uncertainty quantification, machine learning for engineering, and finite element analysis. I am particularly interested in developing physics-informed and data-driven methods for reliability assessment, performance degradation analysis, and fatigue life prediction of complex engineering systems.

I received my Doctor of Engineering in Electronic Information from Fudan University in 2026. My doctoral research focused on performance degradation mechanisms and multiaxial fatigue life prediction methods for civil aircraft door mechanism systems. Before joining Fudan University, I received my Master of Aeronautical Engineering and Bachelor of Safety Engineering from Northwestern Polytechnical University.

My recent research integrates continuum damage mechanics, Bayesian inference, surrogate modeling, multibody dynamics, and finite element simulation to model fatigue damage and reliability under complex loading conditions and multi-source uncertainty. I have also worked on progressive damage simulation, additive manufacturing structures, defect-tolerant design, and reliability analysis of aerospace mechanisms.

My work has been published in journals including *International Journal of Fatigue*, *Aerospace Science and Technology*, *Engineering Failure Analysis*, and *Propulsion and Power Research*. I have received several academic honors, including Outstanding Graduate of Fudan University, Outstanding Doctoral Student Academic Scholarship, and Best Paper Awards from Shanghai Aeronautics Academy.

I welcome research collaborations in computational mechanics, fatigue and fracture, reliability analysis, uncertainty quantification, finite element modeling, and AI-assisted engineering simulation.
